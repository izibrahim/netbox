## NetBox Organizational Data

### Tenant Groups & Tenants

**Tenant Groups** are logical groupings of tenants. You organize tenants into groups based on business units or customer types. Example: a group called "Corporate Division" that holds all departments.

**Tenants** represent business logic — they are the actual entities assigned to infrastructure. Depending on your organization type:
- **Enterprise** → HR, Finance, IT Operations (internal departments)
- **Service Provider** → individual customers

So the relationship is: Tenant Group contains multiple Tenants.

---

### Regions (Hierarchical)

Regions are geographical groupings and support a **parent-child hierarchy**. You can go as deep as needed:

```
North America          ← Continent (parent)
  └── United States    ← Country (child)
        └── New York   ← State/City (grandchild)
```

The tutorial created 42 regions across multiple continents, countries, and cities.

---

### Site Groups & Sites

**Site Groups** are logical categories for grouping sites. Examples from the tutorial: Corporate Office, Branch Office, Data Center, R&D Lab, Training Center.

**Site** is one of the most important objects in NetBox. It typically represents a **physical building**. Every device must belong to a site. The tutorial created 21 sites mapped to regions, site groups, and tenants.

---

### Locations

Locations represent a **specific area within a site** — more granular than a site. Examples: server room, floor, rack row, building section.

Key rule: **Site is mandatory** when creating a location.

```
Site: NYC Headquarters
  └── Location: 4th Floor Server Room
```

The tutorial created 94 locations spread across 21 sites.

---

### Full Hierarchy at a Glance

```
Region (North America)
  └── Site Group (Corporate Office)
        └── Site (NYC HQ)
              └── Location (Server Room)
                    └── Rack → Device

Tenant Group (Corporate Division)
  └── Tenant (IT Operations)
        └── assigned to Site / Location / Device
```
